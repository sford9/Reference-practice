Step By Step To Creating Websites on Github

Step One: Creating New Repository

In the top right corner click the plus sign next to your profile photo, select the new repository.

Step Two: Name it

Github will ask you to name your repository, give it a name.

Step Three: Initialize Repository

Check the box to "initialize this repository with a README".

Step Four: Upload Files

Select the "Upload Files" option.

Step Five: Completeing File Upload

Make sure you have all of your documents in one folder. Keep your assets and styles in seperate folders within your main folder. Click and drag all documents into "Drag Files here to add them to your repository. All of your files should now show up on the github website.

Step Six: Commiting To The Master

After uploading your files select the "commit directly to the master" box then click "Commit Changes" to complete your repository.

Step Seven: Begin the Publishing Process

Now that your files are completely uploaded, Click the settings button on the screen.

Step Eight: Change the Source

To publish your site scroll down in the settings to "Github Pages" find the source section, click the drop down menu and change your source to "Master Branch".

Step Nine: View Your Site

Now your site is published, click the link given to see your website!
